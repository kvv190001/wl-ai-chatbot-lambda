5. How does the RAG System enhance AI accuracy?
- The RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) System enhances prompt accuracy and context grounding using domain-specific knowledge bases. Instead of relying solely on the LLM's training data, RAG retrieves relevant information from your enterprise knowledge bases before generating responses. This ensures answers are grounded in your organization's actual data, policies, and documentation, significantly reducing hallucinations and improving accuracy.

- The Security Layer uses both rule-based and LLM-based scanners (including Llama Guard) for Data Loss Prevention (DLP), compliance checks, and real-time risk mitigation at both input and output levels. Every prompt and response is scanned before being processed or returned to users, ensuring comprehensive protection.

7. How does IAM and Access Control work?
- The WL GenAI Layer enforces user-level permissions and secure API endpoint management through integrated Identity and Access Management (IAM). This ensures that sensitive data and powerful AI capabilities are only available to authorized personnel, maintaining security and compliance.

12. What is Data Loss Prevention (DLP) in the context of AI?
- DLP in the WL GenAI Layer prevents sensitive data from being exposed through AI interactions. The system scans prompts for personally identifiable information (PII), financial data, credentials, proprietary information, and other sensitive content before sending them to LLMs. It also scans AI responses to ensure no sensitive data is inadvertently included in outputs. This protects against data leakage through AI channels.

13. How does the platform prevent AI hallucinations?
- The WL GenAI Layer reduces hallucinations through multiple mechanisms: RAG ensures responses are grounded in verified enterprise data rather than the model's training data, output validation checks responses for factual consistency, confidence scoring flags uncertain responses for human review, and citation tracking ensures claims can be traced back to source documents. This multi-layered approach significantly improves output reliability.

14. What is prompt tuning and how does it work?
- Prompt tuning in the No-Code Console allows you to optimize how questions are sent to AI models. 15. How does model chaining work?
- Model chaining allows you to route requests through multiple AI models in sequence, with each model performing a specific task. For example, one model might classify the request, another retrieves relevant context, a third generates the response, and a fourth validates the output. This orchestration enables sophisticated AI workflows that leverage the strengths of different models while maintaining control and quality.

On-premises deployment runs models within your own infrastructure, providing maximum data control and compliance for highly sensitive environments. The WL GenAI Layer supports both, allowing you to choose based on your security requirements, compliance needs, and use case sensitivity.
